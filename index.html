About Us

WHO WE ARE I-HE Nigeria is a community based organization that works to ensure promotion and protection of human right, provision of sexual reproductive health services, and increase in access to and demand for HIV prevention and treatment services among marginalized population particularly men who have sex with men, LGBTQI, sex workers, adolescent and young people, key population adolescent and young people living with HIV/AIDS. I-HE Nigeria was founded in the year 2019 under the Heartland Alliance green house model to lead HIV response for men who have sex with men in Cross River State. I-HE Nigeria is fully registered with the Cooperate Affairs Commission Nigeria in February, 2021.

WHAT WE DO: I-HE Nigeria implements activities that are targeted at improving access to health care services, promoting and protecting human rights among men who have sex with men, LGBTQI, sex workers, adolescent and young people, key population adolescent and young people living with HIV/AIDS.

OUR TEAM: Board of Trustee Our organization consist of Board of Trustee that is made up of 5 members who have experienced across HIV, TB and Malaria developmental space. The Chairman, Secretary and 3 members constitute this board and also serve as the governing body of the organization. The board of trustee without prejudice is expected to ensure that the policies are implemented accordingly by the organization and also oversees its overall activity

Management Team: Our organization consist of a managerial team of 4 staff. The Executive Director who oversees day to day running of the organisation, the Program Manager who leads, and coordinate projects implementation and management, the financial/admin officer who is responsible for preparation of budgets, financial reports, administration and funds disbursements. The Monitoring and evaluation officer who leads and coordinate the system information unit of the organization

WHERE WE WORK: Geographical Coverage Nigeria. I-HE Nigeria is currently covering Cross River State

TARGET POPULATION: Marginalized population  Men who have sex with men  MSM Sex workers  Sexual minorities (LGBTQ)  Indigent and vulnerable people  Adolescent and young people living with HIV/AIDS
